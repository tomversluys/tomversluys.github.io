Responses to war: Time-series modeling using Google Trends, gold prices, and stock market volatility

I investigate whether an interest in global conflict, indicated by Google search behaviour, is associated with greater interest in God and religion, changing prices of safe haven assets (gold), and more erratic behaviour on the stock market (volatility index, or VIX).

Data:

  • Google trends
  • NASDAQ and Yfinance APIs

Tools:

  • Python
  • SQL
  • Google Cloud (Big Query)

Statistical methods:

  • Vector autoregression

The monthly change in war interest is significantly correlated with monthly changes in religiosity, but not with changes in gold prices or stock market volatility.

We can tentatively infer, then, that as people become more aware of global conflict, interest in religion also increases.
